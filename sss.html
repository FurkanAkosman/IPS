badge How can I become a member of IPS?

expand_more

Membership applications open at the beginning of each academic year through our official website. We welcome students who are eager to collaborate and innovate.

The process typically involves filling out an online form where you can highlight your skills and interests. We do not require a specific GPA; passion is our priority.

paid Do you provide financial support for projects?

expand_more

Yes. We provide seed capital, technical mentorship, and resource support (such as 3D printers and lab access) to selected projects.

Proposals are reviewed by our academic board every semester. Funding priority is given to interdisciplinary teams with a clear prototype plan.

flag What is the core mission of IPS?

expand_more

Our mission is to bring together students, academics, and professionals from various disciplines to promote collaborative projects. We aim to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application.

diversity_3 Do I need a specific major to join?

expand_more

Absolutely not. IPS is open to participants from all disciplines—Engineering, Arts, Psychology, Law, Business, and more. We believe that the best ideas emerge from diverse perspectives.

event How can I follow your events?

expand_more

We organize weekly workshops, guest speaker sessions, and hackathons. All events are announced on our 'Events' calendar page and our social media channels.

groups How do I find teammates for my project idea?

expand_more

IPS hosts "Idea Pitch Nights" at the start of every month. This is a dedicated time for members to present their concepts and recruit teammates. Additionally, our Discord server has a 'team-building' channel specifically for this purpose.

school Is mentorship available for new members?

expand_more

Yes. Each project team is assigned an academic advisor from relevant faculty. We also have a peer-mentorship program where senior members guide new recruits through their first semester.

schedule What is the expected time commitment?

expand_more

While it varies by role, active members typically dedicate 3-5 hours per week. This includes weekly general meetings and project-specific work sessions. We value consistency but understand academic workloads.

public Can non-university students attend events?

expand_more

Many of our large-scale seminars and hackathons are open to the public, though registration is required. Internal workshops and project meetings are generally reserved for registered members.
