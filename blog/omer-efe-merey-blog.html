How Do Environmental Factors Influence the Behavior and Reproduction of Guppies?

By Ömer Efe Merey • 02 Nov 2025

Abstract

This article explores how environmental factors—especially light and water conditions—affect the behavior and breeding of guppies (Poecilia reticulata). It combines personal aquarium observations with John A. Endler's 1987 findings, showing how these fish reflect broader ecological and evolutionary principles.

1. Introduction

Guppies are widely used in aquaria and research for their ease of breeding and sensitivity to environmental change. I am Ömer Efe Merey, a second-year Molecular Biology and Genetics student at Istanbul Sabahattin Zaim University. My interest began with breeding my own guppies and evolved into an academic focus on animal experimentation and behavioral biology.

2. Background: Biology of Guppies

  • Origin: South America; warm freshwater habitats.
  • Temperature range: ~26 °C; pH 6.8–7.6.
  • Livebearers; gestation ≈ 30 days; monthly birth cycles.
  • Gravid spot darkens before birth; fry separation prevents cannibalism.

3. Aquarium Conditions and Maintenance

  • Stocking ≈ 5 L water per guppy; air pump, heater, filter required.
  • Live plants improve water quality; lava rock supports growth.
  • Supplements: conditioners, bacterial cultures, fertilizers.
  • Weekly ¼ water change with dechlorinated water.

4. Scientific Findings: Endler (1987)

Predation and light intensity modulate male courtship. Low light increases displays; bright light or predator presence reduces displays and increases "sneak copulation."

5. Observations

Activity and courtship were higher in low-to-moderate light. Bright light increased caution and hiding. Softer lighting correlated with more fry.

6. Discussion

Guppies show adaptive behavior depending on light, temperature, and risk. Even in controlled aquaria, ecological balance can be observed.

Conclusion

Balanced lighting, stable temperature and chemistry, and structural enrichment improve welfare and reproduction.
