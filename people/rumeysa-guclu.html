Areas of Interest

Furkan’s research interests center on neurotechnology, brain–computer interfaces, and computational neuroscience...

IPS Contributions

As the founder of the Interdisciplinary Project Society (IPS), Furkan has played a leading role in establishing a collaborative ecosystem that connects students from biology, neuroscience, engineering, and computer science. He designed the IPS event structure, including “NeuroArt Workshops,” “Science Speed Networking,” and “AI vs Human Problem-Solving Challenge.” He has also mentored members in project writing (BAP, TÜSEB, COST) and initiated partnerships with research labs and academic mentors. His coordination of IPS communication channels and AI-based workflow automation through Google AI Studio has significantly improved project efficiency and visibility.

Future Aspirations

Furkan aims to pursue advanced research in neuroengineering, focusing on real-time brain decoding and AI-assisted cognitive mapping. His long-term vision is to develop hybrid neurotechnological systems that can translate neural activity into meaningful visual or behavioral outputs, enhancing both medical diagnostics and human–computer interaction. Within IPS, he plans to expand international collaboration networks and establish a mentorship bridge between early-stage researchers and experienced scientists across disciplines.
